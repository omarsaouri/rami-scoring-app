# DESIGN.md — Visual & UX Direction

## Feel

Warm, social, unmistakably Moroccan without tourist cliché. The green of a card table, the warmth of mint tea, geometric nods to zellige tilework — kept modern and functional.

## Color

- **Primary action:** rich emerald green
- **Background:** dark by default (night game sessions), with a warm cream light alternative for daytime
- Score states: green = winning, red = danger zone, skull icon = eliminated

## Typography

- **Display / headings:** clean geometric sans-serif with Arabic support (e.g., IBM Plex Arabic or Noto Sans Arabic)
- **Scores / numbers:** monospaced or tabular-figure font so columns align
- Score numbers are the **largest, most prominent element** on screen

## Core UX Principles

- **The scoreboard is the app.** It loads first and loads fast (under 1 second).
- **Score entry must be faster than writing on paper** — under 5 seconds per player per round, or the app fails its core promise.
- **No sign-up.** Works fully offline from first launch.

## Language

- Trilingual UI: Darija (Arabic script), French, English.
- Keep Moroccan terms untranslated: **Tirsi, Suivi, Vierge, Tallage**.
- Variant names players recognize: "Simple", "71", "71 Bla Joker".
- A built-in card-value reference sheet, accessible during a game.

## Mode-Specific UI

- In **71 Bla Joker**, clearly show which 2s are jokers this round with a visual indicator.

## Open Naming/Style Questions

- App name: "Rami Score" / "Rami Marocain" / "L7sab" (الحساب).
- Darija script: standard Arabic script vs. Latinized Darija (how most Moroccans type) — consider supporting both.
- iPad: a landscape scoreboard could be the best way to play; layout needs thought.
